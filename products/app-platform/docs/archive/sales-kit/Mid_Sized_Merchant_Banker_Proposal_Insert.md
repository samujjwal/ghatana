Status: Tailored customer-facing insert for a realistic Nepal merchant banker prospect profile  
Last revised: March 1, 2026

Assumed prospect profile: SEBON-registered merchant banker handling recurring public issues, coordinating across ASBA and depository workflows, and carrying manual filing and evidence-pack overhead.  
Canonical claims source: [Customer_Facing_Claims_Pack.md](../Customer_Facing_Claims_Pack.md)

---

## Prospect Profile Assumption

This insert is tailored for a realistic mid-sized Nepal merchant banker prospect:

- already licensed and active in issue-management workflows,
- dealing with recurring filing-pack preparation,
- carrying manual pressure around due diligence, allotment support, and issue-close reporting,
- needing tighter operational control without a full infrastructure replacement.

This is a profile-based insert, not a document for a named institution. It does not assume facts about a specific merchant banker.

---

## What Pain Is Most Likely Acute

For this buyer profile, the most likely friction is concentrated in:

- prospectus and filing-pack completeness,
- due-diligence evidence tracking,
- ASBA and application reconciliation,
- post-issue close timing,
- repeatable but not fully uniform reporting to the Board and exchange.

These are the workflows where manual document handling and cross-party coordination create the most operational drag.

---

## Why Siddhanta Fits This Profile

Siddhanta is a strong fit when a merchant banker wants deadline-aware execution and cleaner auditability across the entire issue lifecycle.

The strongest value points for this profile are:

- filing-pack orchestration tied to clause-backed submission duties,
- due-diligence evidence trails that stay linked to the issue record,
- configurable issue-close reporting with the current `7-day` timing anchor,
- better exception visibility across ASBA, allotment, and issue-close operations.

This gives the prospect a safer path to better execution quality without assuming every live filing template is fixed.

---

## Control Story For This Prospect

The platform can be positioned around a merchant-banker-specific control narrative:

- merchant bankers are legally allowed to perform issue management, underwriting, and share registration / registrar functions (`Ref: LCA-005`, `Ref: LCA-006`)
- the current filing baseline includes a prescribed prospectus structure and an issuance/sales-manager due diligence certificate (`Ref: LCA-025`)
- after completing sale / distribution / allotment, the Board must be informed within `7 days` with the prescribed issue-close particulars (`Ref: LCA-026`)

That is the right message for a mid-sized merchant banker because it ties operational value to current clause-backed timing and filing duties.

---

## Recommended Sales Angle

Lead with:

1. cleaner filing-pack preparation,
2. better evidence traceability,
3. fewer deadline risks,
4. more controlled issue-close execution.

Use phrasing like:

- “This platform gives a merchant banker a more controlled issue workflow by tying the filing pack, due-diligence record, and issue-close reporting flow to current clause-backed duties.”
- “The value is not just faster documents; it is cleaner evidence, better deadline control, and lower manual filing risk.”
- “Issue-close reporting remains configurable because the timing is fixed but live template mechanics still need current-process validation.”

Avoid:

- claiming a fixed modern portal form unless it is refreshed from the live filing channel,
- implying every issue follows an identical submission path across all deal types.

---

## Recommended Pilot For This Prospect

1. Prospectus and due-diligence submission-pack workflow
2. Application / ASBA reconciliation and exception handling
3. Issue-close reporting and allotment support within the current `7-day` clock

This pilot proves value where merchant bankers feel it fastest: cleaner filings, fewer avoidable deadline misses, and better post-issue control.
