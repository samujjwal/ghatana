Status: Tailored customer-facing insert for a realistic Nepal full-service broker prospect profile  
Last revised: March 1, 2026

Assumed prospect profile: SEBON-registered full-service broker operating a brokerage-led business with depository-participant workflows, margin-service ambition, and manual reporting pressure.  
Canonical claims source: [Customer_Facing_Claims_Pack.md](../Customer_Facing_Claims_Pack.md)

---

## Prospect Profile Assumption

This insert is tailored for a realistic mid-market Nepal broker prospect:

- already operating as a regulated broker,
- likely managing both brokerage and depository-participant workflows,
- facing recurring post-trade and reporting workload,
- needing better control evidence before scaling into higher-service offerings.

This is a profile-based insert, not a document for a named institution. It avoids inventing prospect-specific facts that are not verified.

---

## What Pain Is Most Likely Acute

For this buyer profile, the most likely pain is concentrated in:

- client-money control and auditability,
- contract-note and post-trade communication timing,
- quarterly return preparation,
- fee and ledger accuracy,
- margin workflow readiness,
- exception resolution during busy trading periods.

These are the areas where manual back-office operations tend to create the highest compliance and operational drag.

---

## Why Siddhanta Fits This Profile

Siddhanta is a strong fit when the broker wants to improve control quality before taking on more operational complexity.

The strongest value points for this profile are:

- broker control workflows tied to clause-backed operating duties,
- fee, levy, and transaction-charge logic tied to current scheduled rules,
- margin-threshold logic aligned to current clause-backed minimums,
- evidence-pack generation for regulator-facing review and internal control audits.

This gives the prospect a safer modernization path than replacing everything at once.

---

## Control Story For This Prospect

The platform can be positioned around a tight broker-specific control narrative:

- segregated client-funds handling and restricted-use cash controls (`Ref: LCA-012`)
- prompt trade-completion information and next-day contract notes (`Ref: LCA-012`, `Ref: LCA-021`)
- Board-specified quarterly return timing within `30 days` (`Ref: LCA-024`)
- clause-backed brokerage slabs, Board levy, and securities-market transaction fee logic (`Ref: LCA-023`)
- clause-backed margin eligibility and minimum margin thresholds (`Ref: LCA-011`)

That is the right message for a mid-market broker because it focuses on operational risk reduction, not generic transformation language.

---

## Recommended Sales Angle

Lead with:

1. control reliability,
2. reporting timeliness,
3. reduced manual exception handling,
4. readiness for higher-service broker operations.

Use phrasing like:

- “This platform helps a full-service broker tighten the controls regulators actually care about: client-funds handling, contract-note timing, quarterly returns, and margin governance.”
- “The fee and margin logic is tied to current clause-backed rules rather than manually maintained spreadsheets.”
- “The value is not just automation; it is cleaner evidence, faster reporting, and fewer avoidable control breaks.”

Avoid:

- promising a clause-verified daily-reconciliation legal clock,
- implying every high-impact decision is automated,
- implying that live regulator filing templates are static.

---

## Recommended Pilot For This Prospect

1. Client-money and post-trade control workflow
2. Contract-note and reporting evidence pack
3. Margin monitoring and margin-call orchestration

This pilot fits a broker that wants measurable control improvement without taking on a full platform replacement in the first phase.
