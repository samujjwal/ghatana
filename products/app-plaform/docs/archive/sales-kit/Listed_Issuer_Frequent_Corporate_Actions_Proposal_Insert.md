# Project Siddhanta: Listed Issuer With Frequent Corporate Actions Insert

Version: 1.0  
Status: Tailored customer-facing insert for a realistic listed-issuer prospect profile with recurring corporate-action activity  
Last revised: March 1, 2026

Assumed prospect profile: a listed Nepal issuer with recurring bonus, rights, dividend, board-notice, or record-date workflows and repeated disclosure-calendar pressure.  
Canonical claims source: [Customer_Facing_Claims_Pack.md](../Customer_Facing_Claims_Pack.md)

---

## Prospect Profile Assumption

This insert is tailored for a realistic listed issuer that:

- has ongoing periodic and event-driven disclosure obligations,
- runs frequent corporate-action or record-date workflows,
- carries governance pressure around board decisions, notices, and shareholder communications,
- needs stronger deadline discipline and evidence traceability.

This is a profile-based insert, not a document for a named company. It does not assume facts about a specific issuer.

---

## What Pain Is Most Likely Acute

For this buyer profile, the most likely operational pressure sits in:

- price-sensitive disclosure timing,
- book-closure and record-date notices,
- AGM and board-pack coordination,
- shareholder-record support,
- repeated corporate-action communication and evidence management.

These are the workflows where delays or process gaps can become a governance and listing-risk issue rather than just an admin issue.

---

## Why Siddhanta Fits This Profile

Siddhanta is a strong fit when a listed issuer wants a tighter disclosure-control layer rather than a generic investor-relations tool.

The strongest value points for this profile are:

- disclosure workflows tied to clause-backed timing duties,
- record-date and book-closure orchestration,
- better visibility into event-driven and periodic filing deadlines,
- cleaner evidence packs for regulator and exchange interactions.

This gives the prospect a practical way to reduce deadline risk and improve control visibility around recurring corporate actions.

---

## Control Story For This Prospect

The platform can be positioned around an issuer-specific control narrative:

- listed issuers must provide book-closure / record-date notices, meeting packs, progress reports, and shareholder-record disclosures on defined timelines (`Ref: LCA-027`)
- listing agreements must cover price-sensitive and financial-information dissemination (`Ref: LCA-028`)
- late price-sensitive or annual / semiannual reporting can trigger listing suspension (`Ref: LCA-028`)

That is the right message for an issuer with frequent corporate actions because it connects the workflow pain directly to listing-risk and governance exposure.

---

## Recommended Sales Angle

Lead with:

1. disclosure timing discipline,
2. lower suspension-risk exposure,
3. cleaner governance workflow,
4. better corporate-action notice control.

Use phrasing like:

- “This platform helps a listed issuer manage the real control problem: repeated disclosures, notices, and governance deadlines that can become listing-risk if they slip.”
- “The value is stronger control over price-sensitive notices, record dates, and shareholder-facing governance packs.”
- “Corporate-action workflows become deadline-aware, evidence-backed, and easier to audit.”

Avoid:

- claiming a fixed live exchange submission mechanic without current notice-level revalidation,
- implying every issuer uses the same live filing template.

---

## Recommended Pilot For This Prospect

1. Price-sensitive and periodic disclosure calendar
2. Corporate-action / book-closure / record-date workflow
3. Shareholder-record and board / AGM evidence-pack generation

This pilot is the clearest way to show fewer missed deadlines, stronger governance discipline, and cleaner disclosure execution for an active issuer.
