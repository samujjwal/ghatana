## Purpose

This appendix maps legal or quasi-legal claims in the active Project Siddhanta documents to the best currently identified authority source.

It is not a legal opinion. It is a traceability layer intended to:

- separate clause-verified claims from document-level references,
- identify where wording is supported only as a project policy baseline,
- flag statements that still need exact clause extraction before being treated as hard legal requirements.

**Namespace Note:** The numeric IDs in this appendix, such as `LCA-001` through `LCA-032`, are legal-claim identifiers for source-backed assertions in the broader documentation set. They are separate from the semantic epic/control identifiers in [epics/COMPLIANCE_CODE_REGISTRY.md](/home/samujjwal/Developments/finance/epics/COMPLIANCE_CODE_REGISTRY.md), such as `LCA-AUDIT-001` and `ASR-SEC-001`.

---

## Evidence Status

- `clause-verified`
  - The claim is tied to a named clause, regulation number, or section in a primary source.
- `document-verified`
  - The source document is verified, but the exact legal clause or numbered obligation is not yet pinned in this appendix.
- `project-policy`
  - The statement is a deliberate product or documentation policy, not yet traced to a binding legal clause in this appendix.
- `pending-clause-extraction`
  - The relevant authority document is known, but the exact clause still needs to be extracted before the claim should be treated as a hard legal requirement.

## Immediate Cleanup Rules Derived From This Appendix

1. If a claim is only `project-policy`, documents should avoid phrasing it as “required by regulator” unless that clause is later verified.
2. If a claim is `pending-clause-extraction`, documents may keep the operational requirement, but should avoid exact legal-certification language.
3. If a claim is `document-verified`, documents should cite the source document and keep the wording at the level the source actually supports.
4. `clause-verified` claims are the only ones that should be treated as hard legal anchors in customer-facing requirement traceability.
