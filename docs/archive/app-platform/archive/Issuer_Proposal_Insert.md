## Why This Matters For Issuers

For listed issuers, the risk is not only operational overhead. It is disclosure timing, filing discipline, and suspension exposure.

The current documentation set now treats issuer workflows as a control problem:

- event-driven disclosure,
- book-closure and record-date notice timing,
- board and AGM documentation,
- periodic report delivery,
- defensible evidence for filings and shareholder communications.

---

## What Siddhanta Delivers

Siddhanta's disclosure and filing workflows are governed by **versioned Rule Packs** — disclosure timelines, price-sensitive event triggers, and filing schema are all externalized from the core platform and configurable per jurisdiction. When SEBON or NEPSE updates a disclosure requirement, only the Rule Pack changes, not the platform. This design means the same platform can serve issuers across multiple jurisdictions as the platform expands regionally.

For issuers, Siddhanta focuses on:

- issuer filing calendar management,
- disclosure workflow orchestration,
- corporate-action and record-date controls,
- shareholder-record and ownership-summary support,
- evidence-pack generation for regulator and exchange interactions.

The goal is to reduce missed deadlines and improve confidence in price-sensitive and governance-related disclosures.

---

## Clause-Backed Issuer Disclosure Anchors

The current documentation set includes clause-backed anchors for issuer-side disclosure:

- listed issuers must provide book-closure / record-date notices, meeting packs, progress reports, and shareholder-record disclosures on defined timelines under the listing agreement (`Ref: LCA-027`)
- listing agreements must cover price-sensitive and financial-information dissemination (`Ref: LCA-028`)
- failure to provide price-sensitive information or annual / semiannual reports on time can trigger listing suspension (`Ref: LCA-028`)

These are strong anchors for an issuer workflow platform because they tie the system to concrete timing and disclosure obligations rather than generic “IR automation.”

---

## Corporate Actions And Primary-Market Support

Where the issuer is also running a public issue or post-issue workflow:

- the current filing baseline includes a prescribed prospectus structure and an issuance/sales-manager due diligence certificate (`Ref: LCA-025`)
- post-issue close reporting to the Board currently carries a `7-day` timing anchor (`Ref: LCA-026`)

This means the same platform can support both:

- ongoing listed-issuer disclosures, and
- issue-specific filing and closeout duties.

## Safe External Positioning

Use:

- “The platform helps listed issuers manage disclosure timing because book-closure, meeting, progress, and price-sensitive notices sit on defined timelines.”
- “Late issuer reporting is a real control risk because missing price-sensitive or periodic disclosures can create suspension exposure.”
- “The platform improves filing discipline through workflow controls, deadline visibility, and evidence-pack generation.”

Avoid:

- claiming that every live filing template is fixed or uniform across issuer categories,
- claiming an exact NEPSE submission mechanic without current notice-level revalidation.
